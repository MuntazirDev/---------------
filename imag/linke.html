By B&H Photo's

Streamline productivity with the U2723QE 27" 16:9 4K UHD HDR IPS Monitor with USB Type-C Docking from Dell. Featuring a 27" In-Plane Switching (IPS) panel, this monitor provides a 4K UHD 3840 x 2160 resolution for ample detail. Connect via USB Type-C to take advantage of data, video, and up to 90W of power delivery via a single cable. An integrated hub comprised of two USB 3.2 Gen 2 Type-C and five Type-A ports accommodates a host of accessories, and an RJ45 Ethernet port enables wired network connections. Plus, amp up productivity with a range of features including PbP and PiP modes, window partition presets, automatic KVM switching, wake-on-power and wake-on-LAN, and more.

In addition to a USB Type-C input, this monitor can also connect to hosts via DisplayPort and HDMI inputs. View content in vibrant detail thanks to a 2000:1 static contrast ratio, support for 1.07 billion colors, and 100% Rec 709, 100% sRGB, and 98% DCI-P3 coverage. An 8 ms (GTG) response time can be overdriven to 5 ms and, coupled with a 60 Hz refresh rate, allows for smooth video playback. Stay comfortable during long viewing sessions with a 400 nits brightness, 178° horizontal and vertical viewing angles, and blue light filtering. Integrated cable management and a 100 x 100mm VESA mounting pattern make deployment a breeze.

    How is this different from the Dell U2720Q?

    How is this different from the Dell U2720Q?
    Asked by: Jason
    Key improvements in display quality per the specs on Dell's website include increasing contrast ratio from 1300:1 to 2000:1, brightness from 350 to 400 nits, and coverage of DCI-P3 from 95% to 98%. Another notable improvement over the predecessor is the built in Multi-Stream Transport (MST) splitter. This allows you to connect multiple monitors in a daisy chain from a single DisplayPort (including USB-C alternate mode) connection to the computer. This feature really helps the scenario of connecting a laptop using a single cable in industries where multiple monitors are the norm. There are two caveats to be aware of here: - DisplayPort 1.4a doesn't have the bandwidth to do two 4K displays at 10-bit color and 60Hz uncompressed. You need to use Display Stream Compression (DSC) to achieve multiple 4K 10-bit displays. While supposedly visually lossless, it's not completely lossless for unavoidable technical reasons. - Apple steadfastly refuses to add MST support to their graphics stack despite the hardware capability as demonstrated running Windows under Boot Camp.
    Answered by: Paul
    Date published: 2023-12-10
